# Key Activities (KA)

> *"The Key Activities Building Block describes the most important things a company must do to make its business model work."*
> — Osterwalder & Pigneur, p. 36

## Definition

Key activities are the most important actions a company must perform to operate successfully. Like key resources, they are required to create and deliver the value proposition, reach markets, maintain customer relationships, and earn revenues. Key activities vary depending on the type of business model.

## Key questions

- What key activities do our value propositions require?
- Our distribution channels?
- Our customer relationships?
- Our revenue streams?

## Categories of key activities

### Production
Activities related to designing, making, and delivering a product in substantial quantities and/or of superior quality. Dominates the business models of manufacturing firms.

**Example:** Dell — supply chain management

### Problem solving
Activities related to coming up with new solutions to individual customer problems. Dominates consultancies, hospitals, and other service organizations. Requires knowledge management and continuous training.

**Example:** McKinsey — problem solving and advisory

### Platform / Network
Business models where a platform is the key resource are dominated by platform- and network-related activities. Includes networking, matchmaking, software, and brands.

**Examples:**
- eBay: continuously develops and maintains the marketplace (ebay.com)
- Visa: manages the transaction platform for cardholders, merchants, and banks
- Microsoft: manages the interface between other vendors' software and the Windows platform

Key activities here: platform management, service provisioning, and platform promotion.

## Questions to ask

1. What type of business is this — producer, problem solver, or platform?
2. What are the three to five activities without which the value proposition cannot be delivered?
3. Which activities create the most value — and which consume the most resources?
4. Which activities are unique/proprietary vs. generic?
5. Which activities could be outsourced to partners?

## Connections to other building blocks

- **VP**: KA are the actions that enable delivery of the value proposition
- **KR**: Activities require resources — the two are tightly linked
- **KP**: Activities that are not core competencies can be performed by partners
- **C$**: KA are typically one of the largest cost drivers in the business model
