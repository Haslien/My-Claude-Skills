# Revenue Streams (R$)

> *"The Revenue Streams Building Block represents the cash a company generates from each Customer Segment."*
> — Osterwalder & Pigneur, p. 30

## Definition

Revenue streams are the arteries of the business model. A company must ask: *For what value is each customer segment truly willing to pay?* Each segment can yield one or more revenue streams with different pricing mechanisms.

Costs must be subtracted from revenues to calculate earnings.

## Key questions

- For what value are customers *really* willing to pay?
- What do they currently pay for?
- How are they currently paying?
- How would they prefer to pay?
- How much does each revenue stream contribute to overall revenues?

## Two types of revenue

| Type | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **Transaction revenues** | One-time customer payments |
| **Recurring revenues** | Ongoing payments to deliver the VP or provide post-purchase support |

## Revenue models

### Asset sale
The most widely understood — selling ownership rights to a physical product. Example: Amazon sells books; Fiat sells cars.

### Usage fee
Payment based on use of the service. The more it is used, the more the customer pays. Example: telecom operator (minutes), hotel (nights), package delivery (per package).

### Subscription fee
Continuous access to a service in exchange for periodic payment. Example: gym (monthly), World of Warcraft (monthly), Nokia's Comes with Music.

### Lending / Renting / Leasing
Temporarily granting exclusive use of an asset for a fee. Gives the lender the advantage of recurring revenues; the renter avoids the full cost of ownership. Example: Zipcar (car by the hour).

### Licensing
Permission to use protected intellectual property in exchange for a licensing fee. Common in the media industry and technology sector. Example: Qualcomm earns licensing fees from patented chip designs.

### Brokerage fees
Revenue from intermediation services between two or more parties. Example: credit card companies take a percentage of each transaction between merchants and buyers. Real estate agents earn commission.

### Advertising
Revenue from fees for advertising a product, service, or brand. Example: media, events, and increasingly software and services (Google, Facebook).

## Pricing mechanisms

### Fixed pricing
| Mechanism | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| List price | Fixed prices per product/service/VP |
| Product feature dependent | Price depends on the number or quality of VP features |
| Customer segment dependent | Price depends on the type and characteristics of the segment |
| Volume dependent | Price depends on the quantity purchased |

### Dynamic pricing
| Mechanism | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| Negotiation | Price negotiated between parties |
| Yield management | Price depends on inventory and time of purchase (hotels, airlines) |
| Real-time market | Price set dynamically based on supply and demand |
| Auctions | Price determined by competitive bidding |

## Questions to ask

1. Is the revenue transactional (one-time purchase) or recurring (subscription/usage)?
2. Would customers prefer a different payment model than what we use today?
3. Is there untapped willingness to pay in the segment?
4. How price-sensitive are customers — are they price-driven or value-driven?
5. Which revenue stream contributes the most? Is it right to focus there?

## Connections to other building blocks

- **CS**: Each segment can have different revenue streams and pricing models
- **VP**: R$ is directly tied to the value customers experience and are willing to pay for
- **C$**: Revenue minus costs = profit — these two define the business's viability
