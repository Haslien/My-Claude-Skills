# Channels (CH)

> *"The Channels Building Block describes how a company communicates with and reaches its Customer Segments to deliver a Value Proposition."*
> — Osterwalder & Pigneur, p. 26

## Definition

Communication, distribution, and sales channels comprise a company's interface with customers. Channels are customer touchpoints that play an important role in the customer experience.

Channels serve several functions:
- Raising awareness about a company's products and services
- Helping customers evaluate the value proposition
- Allowing customers to purchase products and services
- Delivering the value proposition to customers
- Providing post-purchase customer support

## Key questions

- Through which channels do our customer segments want to be reached?
- How are we reaching them now?
- How are our channels integrated?
- Which ones work best?
- Which are most cost-efficient?
- How do they integrate with customers' routines?

## The five channel phases

Channels can cover some or all of these phases:

| Phase | Question |
|-------|----------|
| **1. Awareness** | How do we raise awareness about our products and services? |
| **2. Evaluation** | How do we help customers evaluate our value proposition? |
| **3. Purchase** | How do we allow customers to purchase? |
| **4. Delivery** | How do we deliver the VP to customers? |
| **5. After sales** | How do we provide post-purchase customer support? |

## Channel types

### Own channels (direct)
- Sales force (in-house)
- Website / web sales
- Own stores

Higher margins, but costly to build and operate.

### Own channels (indirect)
- Stores owned or operated by the company

### Partner channels (indirect)
- Wholesale distribution
- Retail
- Partner-owned websites

Lower margins, but broader reach and the ability to leverage partner strengths.

## Direct vs. indirect — the trade-off

| | Direct | Indirect |
|--|--------|----------|
| Margins | Higher | Lower |
| Control | More | Less |
| Cost | Higher (setup + ops) | Lower |
| Reach | Limited by own resources | Scalable via partners |

The key is finding the right mix that creates a great customer experience and maximizes revenues.

## Questions to ask

1. Which channels does the customer already use for similar products/services?
2. Which phases of the buying journey are covered — and which are missing?
3. Is there a gap between awareness and purchase?
4. Do the channels integrate well with each other (e.g. online + physical)?
5. What does each channel cost per customer acquired?

## Connections to other building blocks

- **CS**: Different segments prefer different channels
- **VP**: Channels are the delivery mechanism for the value proposition
- **CR**: Channels are also relationship builders — they shape the customer experience
- **R$**: Channel choice affects costs and pricing
- **KP**: Partner channels are often key partnerships
